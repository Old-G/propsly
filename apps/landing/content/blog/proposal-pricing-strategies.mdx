## Why Pricing Makes or Breaks Your Proposal

You can write the most compelling scope of work in the world, but if your pricing section feels off, the deal stalls. Pricing isn't just about numbers. It's about psychology, framing, and giving your client confidence that they're making the right investment.

After analyzing hundreds of proposals, we've identified five strategies that consistently improve close rates.

## 1. Lead With a Higher Anchor

Always present your most comprehensive option first. When a client sees a $15,000 package before a $7,500 one, the second option feels like a bargain. This is called **price anchoring**, and it works because our brains evaluate prices relative to the first number we see.

Put your premium tier at the top of the pricing table. Even if most clients pick the mid-tier, the anchor reframes their perception of value.

## 2. Offer Three Tiers, Highlight the Middle

The "rule of three" isn't just a writing technique. It's a pricing principle. When you present three options (Basic, Standard, Premium), most buyers gravitate toward the middle. It feels safe, neither too cheap nor too expensive.

Make your middle tier visually prominent. Use a different background color, add a "Recommended" badge, or give it a slightly larger font. Guide the eye where you want it to go.

## 3. Break Down the Investment

A $12,000 project sounds expensive. A $3,000/month engagement over four months feels manageable. Whenever possible, reframe lump sums as monthly or phased payments. This reduces sticker shock and makes it easier for clients to get budget approval.

Include a clear payment schedule in your proposal. Something like:

- 30% upon signing ($3,600)
- 30% at mid-project milestone ($3,600)
- 40% upon delivery ($4,800)

## 4. Show the Cost of Doing Nothing

One of the most overlooked pricing tactics is framing your fee against the cost of inaction. If your client is losing $5,000/month in manual inefficiencies and your project costs $15,000, they break even in three months. That's a compelling ROI story.

Add a brief "cost of the status quo" section before your pricing table. Quantify the pain they're already paying for, whether it's lost revenue, wasted hours, or missed opportunities.

## 5. Use Interactive Pricing Tables

Static pricing is a missed opportunity. When clients can toggle add-ons, adjust quantities, or select packages in an interactive table, they feel ownership over the final number. They're not being told a price; they're building one.

This is exactly why we built interactive pricing blocks into Propsly. Clients can configure their package in real time, which increases engagement and reduces back-and-forth negotiations.

## Put It Into Practice

The best proposals combine great writing with smart pricing psychology. Start by restructuring your next proposal with these five strategies and measure the difference. Small changes in how you present price can lead to significantly higher close rates.
